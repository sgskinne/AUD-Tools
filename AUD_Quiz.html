5 questions on Day 1 material. Not graded — just to see what stuck. Tap an answer for each question, then hit Submit.

1
A patient meets 7 of the 11 DSM-5 AUD criteria in the past 12 months. What severity level does this represent?
✅ Severe. DSM-5 thresholds: 2–3 = Mild, 4–5 = Moderate, 6 or more = Severe. 7 criteria = firmly severe.
2
Which toxic byproduct of alcohol metabolism causes both liver damage AND hangover symptoms?
✅ Acetaldehyde. Ethanol → acetaldehyde (via ADH) → acetate (via ALDH). Acetaldehyde is highly toxic — it damages hepatocytes and causes hangover symptoms. ALDH2 deficiency causes it to build up faster (Asian flush).
3
A standard US drink contains approximately how many grams of pure alcohol?
✅ 14 grams. = 12 oz beer (5%) = 5 oz wine (12%) = 1.5 oz spirits (40%). Many other countries use 10 g. Many real-world drinks contain more than one standard drink.
4
Women typically reach a higher peak BAC than men after the same amount of alcohol. The PRIMARY reason is:
✅ Less total body water. Alcohol distributes into body water. Women average ~55% body water vs. ~68% for men (the Widmark r-constant: 0.55 vs 0.68). Same dose in less water = higher concentration.
5
What percentage of people with AUD are estimated to have some form of alcohol-associated liver disease?
✅ 90–95%. Nearly all AUD patients have some degree of ALD — from reversible fatty liver (steatosis) to irreversible cirrhosis. Early stages can reverse with abstinence; advanced cirrhosis cannot.
